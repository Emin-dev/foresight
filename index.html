Every enemy shows you its move before it happens. Losses are planning failures, not bad luck.

Foresight is a small grid-tactics game in the vein of Into the Breach: at the start of every turn, each enemy's exact next action — where it will step, what it will hit, for how much — is telegraphed on the board before you move. Then you act, and resolution plays out exactly as shown. No hidden rolls, no fudged AI, no lies between the telegraph and the outcome.

This is a craft bet, not a revenue plan

Market research for this concept scored it low — 2/10. The evidence cited in favor of a deterministic tactics game was Into the Breach's critical acclaim and sales, but that evidence belongs to a critically-acclaimed studio with press relationships and Steam's discovery algorithm behind it. None of that transfers to an unbranded clone with no storefront and no press — the research explicitly named this a reference-class fallacy: borrowing a comparable's success numbers for a product that shares only the mechanic, not the distribution.

Foresight is built anyway, as a craft and genre-diversity bet for this catalog — this is the first grid-tactics game shipped so far, and the determinism-proof architecture (see below) was worth building and testing for its own sake. Go in expecting a small, honestly-scoped tactics puzzle, not the next Into the Breach.

The determinism promise — verify it yourself

js/enemies.js's computeIntent() is a pure function of the current board: same input, same output, every time — no randomness, no hidden state. js/run.js's startTurn() calls it before you act, so what you see on the board is locked in. resolveTurn() then executes those exact captured intents against the board after your move — never recomputed against your new position. Read both files directly: there is no branch anywhere that recalculates an enemy's plan once it's been shown to you.

Units & enemies

UnitRole
■ BulwarkSlow, sturdy blocker. Holds a tile enemies can't pass through or occupy. Deals no damage.
▲ RamPushes an adjacent enemy back one tile — if the push is blocked, the enemy takes damage instead.
● StrikerDirect damage in a straight line, up to 3 tiles, if nothing blocks the line.
✦ CrawlerAlways advances toward the nearest objective, then attacks whatever tile it ends up facing.

Repo & tests

Zero dependencies, no build step, no server-side logic. Every game rule is pure logic covered by real Node tests, including two full scripted mission playthroughs (one win, one loss) that assert the exact resulting board state:

node test/board.test.mjs
node test/enemies.test.mjs
node test/units.test.mjs
node test/run.test.mjs
node test/checkout.test.mjs

15 missions across 3 islands (6x6 grid throughout), every one confirmed winnable ahead of time by exhaustive or large-scale randomized search against the real engine — not just assumed.
